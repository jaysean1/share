AI Study Desk

An intelligent solution designed to help children with homework and ease the burden on parents.

Background

Recently, while visiting friends, I witnessed the process of them helping their children with homework. The friends needed to stay with the child throughout, answering questions promptly, guiding patiently, and finally grading the homework.

The entire process lasted nearly two hours, and only then could we sit down to chat. This made me realize that tutoring children with homework nowadays is an extremely arduous task. My friends also told me that schools today are very different from our time:

  • Large Homework Volume: The amount of homework assigned in elementary schools today far exceeds the past. Previously, we could complete homework at school, but now it’s nearly impossible, requiring 2-3 hours at home to finish elementary school homework.
  • High Difficulty: The difficulty of elementary school homework has significantly increased. Some questions are so challenging that even parents struggle to solve them, needing to consult resources or search online.
  • Full Parental Involvement: Nowadays, homework grading primarily relies on parents rather than teachers. Teachers assign homework to be completed at home, parents grade it, and teachers only record the homework status.

During this process, I identified two core issues:

Difficulty

The increased difficulty of homework often requires parents to look up information to complete it.

Exhaustion

Beyond the difficulty, the process is extremely time-consuming and exhausting. After a day’s work, parents must spend another 2-3 hours tutoring their children, which is overwhelming.

This led me to consider whether an intelligent solution could help my friends and their children with homework tutoring. This solution needs to meet the following requirements:

  • Automatic Answering: Can automatically respond to and answer children’s questions, assisting with homework completion.
  • Automatic Recording: Automatically records the entire learning process and generates a final study report.
  • No Additional Hardware: Requires no extra hardware purchases, only a computer and a phone.

Solution Design

Based on the above requirements, I began designing and selecting the specific solution.

Process Design

Solution Description:

  • Frontend: Implemented using Python Tkinter, including a video monitoring interface and a configuration interface.
  • Backend: Implemented using Python Flask, including backend interfaces and interaction with Qwen-Omni-VL. Qwen was chosen as the monitoring model due to its high performance and low cost.
  • Study Assistance: Implemented using QuickTime and Doubao 1.5pro, including video monitoring and screen sharing. Doubao is ByteDance’s latest multimodal model, performing excellently in multiple benchmark tests.

Function Testing

After completing the development of the relevant functions, we conducted actual testing:

Configuration Function

In the configuration interface, monitoring point behaviors can be dynamically customized through configuration. Essentially, it passes monitoring point configuration information to Qwen-Omni-VL via dynamic prompts, allowing it to monitor specified behaviors and return recognized video behaviors and their durations. Supported operations include:

  • Add
  • Modify
  • Delete
  • Save
  • Reset
  • Load: Allows dynamic adjustments without restarting the application each time.

Monitoring Function

The monitoring interface is divided into three areas:

  • Video Input Area: Used to display the monitoring footage. Every 10 seconds, the past video content is sent to Qwen-Omni-VL for recognition, and the results are displayed in the recognition result area. Since only one frame is captured per second, video consumption is minimal.
  • Recognition Result Area: Displays Qwen-Omni-VL’s recognition results. We parse the recognized content and track the duration of various behaviors during the process. The results are presented in a chart for clarity.
  • Study Summary Area: After each study session, the model summarizes and scores the process, generating a study summary report with one click.

Study Assistance

In the study assistance interface, we mainly implemented:

  • Study Monitoring: Monitors study content footage. Unlike the ‘monitoring’ function, here we use the phone’s camera to capture content on the desktop. Since elementary school homework is completed in textbooks rather than on computers, we need the large model to see the study content.
  • Real-Time Voice Interaction: Users can initiate question requests during the study process, and the model analyzes the images and calls Doubao 1.5pro to provide analysis results.

Project Summary

Thus, we achieved automation of the entire study process, enabling 80% of homework tutoring without parental intervention.

Of course, there’s still the remaining 20%, including homework correction and recording. I’ve also considered subsequent optimization plans:

  • Correction Function: Requires text-to-image capabilities to present corrected homework content using prompts and images.
  • Homework Recording: Requires the model’s MCP to connect to external document storage products like Notion or Google Docs, automatically uploading and categorizing all content after the study process.

Currently, the above two functions are not fully implemented in the video. In subsequent optimizations, I will implement these features.
